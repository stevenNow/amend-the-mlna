1. Maximize Property Value & Sale Potential

A high concentration of rentals and investor-owned properties directly lowers **Lender Confidence**. This instability often leads to **lower appraisals** because it signals a less committed community.

  • Fewer rentals supports higher **appraisal values** and better comps.
  • A stable community attracts prime buyers using **conventional financing**.
  • This amendment is a **critical safeguard** for every owner's investment.

2. Ensure Long-Term Stability, Safety, and Accountability

Owner-occupants have a vested, long-term interest in the condition and compliance of the community. High rental turnover creates risks, including less immediate accountability for rule compliance and **documented safety issues.**

  • **Addresses documented police/safety reports** that arise from transient tenancy.
  • **Lower turnover** and more engaged, familiar neighbors.
  • A stable community experiences fewer rule violations.

3. Protect the Lake Area

Without a strong amendment, the unique appeal of our **community lake** makes nearby homes prime targets for investors seeking to run short-term rentals (Airbnbs). This introduces rapid turnover and commercial activity.

  • Prevents commercial operations from degrading our **residential atmosphere**.
  • A proactive ban secures the quality of our shared amenities, like the lake.

What You Can Do: Take Action to Preserve Our Lake

We have the power to amend our Declaration and safeguard the community's future. The process is clear and simple:

  • 1 Draft the necessary legal amendments and language.
  • 2 Achieve the required **Two-Thirds (2/3) affirmative vote** from the membership.
  • 3 Record the approved amendments with the county to make them **official and legally binding.**

That's it. Change starts with us.

Why This Matters: Our Family's Story

When our family moved into this community four years ago, we chose it specifically for the quiet streets, the quality of maintenance, and the sense of stability. We saw it as our forever home, a place where our kids could grow up with familiar neighbors. We're Steve, Tracey, James, Reese, and Blake Nowicki.

In the last year alone we have experienced dog attacks, threats of assault, trespassing, and harassment. We've also witnessed a person being arrested from a rental home and a firearm being confiscated.

This is an experience no family should go through.

We want to work with the MLNA to protect our investment and our community. We hope you will join us in securing this piece of mind for every owner.
